# Privacy Policy for Vicinity

**Last updated: April 8, 2026**

## Overview

Vicinity is designed from the ground up with privacy as a core principle. The app enables peer-to-peer chat over Bluetooth and local Wi-Fi using Apple's MultipeerConnectivity framework. **No data ever leaves your device to a server.**

## Data We Collect

**We collect nothing.** Vicinity does not collect, transmit, store, or share any personal data with any third party, including the developer.

## How the App Works

- All messages are sent directly between devices over Bluetooth or peer-to-peer Wi-Fi
- Messages are stored locally on your device only, using Apple's SwiftData framework
- No internet connection is used at any point
- No accounts, login, or registration required
- No analytics, crash reporting, or tracking of any kind

## Bluetooth and Local Network Usage

Vicinity requests access to:

- **Bluetooth** — to discover nearby devices and deliver scheduled messages when a friend comes within range
- **Local Network** — to establish peer-to-peer connections with nearby devices running Vicinity

These permissions are used solely for communication between devices running Vicinity. No data is transmitted to any external server.

## Data Stored on Your Device

The following data is stored locally on your device and never shared:

- Your chosen display name
- Chat messages you send and receive
- A list of known peers (friends you've connected with)
- Scheduled messages you have composed

You can delete all app data at any time by uninstalling Vicinity.

## Children's Privacy

Vicinity does not knowingly collect any information from anyone, including children under 13.
